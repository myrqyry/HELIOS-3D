# Research Module 2: "Super-Moiré" Engineering in $CrI_3$

## Objective and Contextual Framework

While Research Module 1 establishes the mathematical logic and energetic stability of hopfion crystals in theory, maintaining this crystalline structure physically without relying on bulky external magnetic fields represents a severe engineering challenge. In conventional spintronic systems, thermal fluctuations and boundary annihilation effects naturally induce soliton drift, causing the ordered arrays to eventually dissolve into chaos or single-domain states. To address this fundamental limitation, HELIOS-3D must integrate the February 2026 findings surrounding "Super-Moiré" magnetism in twisted double-bilayer Chromium Triiodide ($CrI_3$).

The primary objective of this module is to leverage the 1.1° "Magic Angle" in $CrI_3$ van der Waals heterostructures to serve as the foundational, self-biasing substrate for hopfion nucleation. The research task involves mapping the "elliptical stability" of individual hopfions strictly confined within these large-scale 300 nm super-moiré textures. Current data robustly suggests that these giant magnetic textures provide the exact "trapping potential" needed to prevent hopfions from drifting out of the active compute area. Consequently, this module aims to provide the first physical evidence for Zero-Field Stability, fundamentally removing the need for external electromagnets in the HELIOS-3D device architecture.

## The 1.1° Magic Angle and the Physics of Super-Moiré Magnetism

Twisted bilayer systems have garnered massive scientific attention since the initial discovery of correlated insulating states and unconventional superconductivity in magic-angle graphene. In two-dimensional magnetic systems like $CrI_3$, the stacking order explicitly dictates the magnetic ground state, allowing researchers to transition the interlayer exchange interaction between ferromagnetic (FM) and antiferromagnetic (AFM) alignments simply through mechanical rotation.

Recent twist-angle-dependent studies, combined with massive large-scale atomistic Monte Carlo simulations, have revealed that competing magnetic interactions—specifically the Dzyaloshinskii-Moriya Interaction (DMI) induced by an $hBN/CrI_3$ interface, magnetic anisotropy, and exchange interactions—produce unique topological textures. As the twist angle increases, the atomic moiré unit cell becomes progressively smaller. However, at a critical threshold, the magnetic texture completely decouples from this small atomic moiré length scale. At the critical twist angle of 1.1°, this decoupling gives rise to massive "super-moiré" magnetic patterns.

Unlike standard moiré wavelengths that strictly diminish as the physical twist angle increases, these emergent super-moiré length scales actually expand, reaching unprecedented, observationally verified dimensions of approximately 300 nm. These expansive 300 nm domains have been successfully mapped using scanning nitrogen-vacancy magnetometry (NVM) deployed on an advanced attoAFM/CFM platform utilizing specialized QZabre diamond tips. The resulting vector field maps identified strongly pinned FM and AFM domains and provided the first experimental observations of AFM Néel-type skyrmions in any twisted 2D magnetic system to date.

## Elliptical Stability, Trapping Potentials, and Zero-Field Anchoring

The emergence of these highly stable 300 nm super-moiré structures is precisely the mechanism required to physically host a HELIOS-3D hopfion crystal. The spatial variation in the exchange and DMI landscapes across the super-moiré lattice acts as a deeply modulated, periodic potential energy well. Metaphorically, the substrate functions as a magnetic "waffle iron," where the geometry of the domain strictly dictates the resting state of the enclosed soliton.

When a hopfion is nucleated within one of these 300 nm domains, it encounters a profound energy barrier at the domain wall. The energetic cost of traversing this super-moiré domain boundary into an adjacent cell is substantially higher than the ambient thermal energy ($k_B T$) at operating temperatures. Consequently, the super-moiré texture provides a robust, naturally occurring trapping potential. Current studies in non-magnetic heterobilayers note that deep moiré potentials efficiently trap interlayer excitons, completely preventing lateral drift. Similarly, in these magnetic analogs, the super-moiré modulation creates a periodic pinning landscape that permanently anchors the topological defect.

A critical research output of this module is mapping the "elliptical stability" of the trapped hopfion. Because the super-moiré domains are not perfectly isotropic, a hopfion localized within a cell will experience asymmetrical confining forces, stretching its ordinarily spherical cross-section into an ellipse. By analyzing the stiffness and restorative forces associated with this elliptical deformation, researchers can quantify the exact thermal resilience of the hopfion node. Proving that the elliptical stability remains high despite thermal noise confirms the viability of the substrate.

The profound second-order insight derived from this module is that the HELIOS-3D architecture inherently achieves Zero-Field Stability. Historically, nearly all functional skyrmion and hopfion lattices required an external biasing magnetic field ($H_{ext}$) to prevent the topological textures from expanding into uniform single-domain states, or unwinding entirely at the sample boundaries.

By engineering the HELIOS-3D substrate utilizing 1.1° twisted double-bilayer $CrI_3$, the intrinsic periodic super-moiré potential strictly restricts the hopfion dimensions through localized DMI variation rather than global field application. The lattice constant $a$ of the hopfion crystal (derived from Module 1) can be explicitly tailored during fabrication to perfectly match the 300 nm periodicity of the super-moiré substrate. This synergistic alignment allows HELIOS-3D to operate completely free of external electromagnets, which in turn vastly reduces continuous power consumption, eliminates magnetic cross-talk between adjacent processing cores, and facilitates ultra-dense microchip integration on a scale previously deemed impossible for 3D spintronics.
