# Evidence lane: Thermodynamic reservoir validation

HELIOS-3D does not treat low-energy inference as a single erasure-bound problem. Candidate reservoirs must be evaluated across the full operation lifecycle: Create, Read, Update, Delete, transport, readout, reset, relaxation, and protocol-dependent dissipation.

### Key references

- **Iizumi 2026, Physica A**: CRUD information-thermodynamic accounting.
- **Ding & Qiu 2026, arXiv**: thermodynamics of quantum reservoir computing.
- **Loss 2026, arXiv**: classical reversible computation by quantum coherence.
- **Liu et al. 2026, PRX / APS Viewpoint**: quantum synchronization visible in joint correlations.

### HELIOS interpretation

A physical reservoir is not validated by accuracy alone. It must show useful predictive information relative to memory burden, non-predictive retained history, irreversible work, readout/reset overhead, and robustness under noise.
