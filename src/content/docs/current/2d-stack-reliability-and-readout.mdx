# Evidence lane: 2D stack reliability and photonic readout

Layered materials are treated as active device constraints, not ideal Lego bricks. HELIOS-3D tracks leakage, tunnelling, electrode roughness, contact transfer length, twist-angle hysteresis, defect-emitter behavior, and optical readout geometry as first-order validation problems.

### Key references

- **Yuan et al. 2026, Nature Materials**: quantum tunnelling and leakage across hBN, MoS₂, and WS₂.
- **Šiškins et al. 2026, Nature Communications**: polymer-free van der Waals assembly of 2D material heterostructures using muscovite (mica) crystals.
- **Yang et al. 2026, Nature**: carrier transfer length in 2D-material transistors.
- **Maffione et al. 2026, Nature Materials**: BN/BN twist-angle anomalous gating.
- **ACS Nano 2026**: defect-induced single-photon emission in ZnPS₃.
- **Huang et al. 2026, Optics Letters**: ultrathin diffractive lens forming an optical needle.
- **You & Magaña-Loaiza et al. 2026, Nature**: quantum statistical plasmonic metacrystals forming allowed/forbidden $g^{(2)}(0)$ coherence bands for room-temperature optical state filtering.

### Passive Room-Temperature Coherence Filtering via Quantum Metacrystals

Non-destructive optical readout of 3D hopfions and spin-wave dynamics at room temperature suffers from thermal optical noise ($g^{(2)}(0) \approx 2$). HELIOS-3D incorporates **quantum statistical plasmonic metacrystal coatings** (You et al., *Nature* 2026) directly onto 2D optoelectronic readout tracks:

* **Meta-Atom Statistical Bands:** Nanoantenna arrays ($200 \times 400\text{ nm}$ gold meta-atoms on $110\text{-nm}$ Au film) generate allowed and forbidden quantum statistical bands in the paraxial near field.
* **Passive Noise Suppression:** Light within allowed bands ($g^{(2)}(0)=1$) propagates without loss, while thermal or superthermal optical noise ($g^{(2)}(0)=1.25, 2.15$) in forbidden bands is automatically filtered and thermalized to the nearest allowed state.
* **Room-Temperature Operation:** Enables passive, uncooled optical signal cleaning for non-destructive magneto-optical readout tracks, eliminating cryogenic cooling requirements for high-SNR photon detection. `[DEMONSTRATED]`
