<ExpandableChunk 
  title="CRUD-Style Information Thermodynamics (Iizumi, Physica A 2026)" 
  summary="Extending Landauer's erasure bound as the Delete-specific limit of a broader CRUD framework."
>
  Iizumi, *Physica A: Statistical Mechanics and its Applications* (2026, DOI: `10.1016/j.physa.2026.131801`), proposes a unified information-thermodynamic framework that treats Landauer's erasure bound as the Delete-specific limit of a broader Create/Read/Update/Delete (CRUD) accounting system. The framework uses nonequilibrium free-energy inequalities to account for transformations of probability distributions over physical memory states. `[DEMONSTRATED]`

  * **Internal Consistency:** The paper reports a sorry-free Lean 4 formalization of the algebraic identities behind the CRUD accounting framework, establishing the internal consistency of the thermodynamic decomposition.
  * **HELIOS Relevance:** Supports HELIOS-3D’s validation philosophy: energy claims must account for the full lifecycle of information operations (reservoir state creation/preparation, physical update/perturbation, readout/measurement, overwrite/reset, and protocol-dependent dissipation) rather than focusing solely on erasure.
</ExpandableChunk>

<ExpandableChunk 
  title="Classical Reversible Computation by Quantum Coherence (Loss, arXiv 2026)" 
  summary="Classical reversible logic mapped to coherent spin dynamics in semiconductor quantum dots."
>
  Loss, *Classical Reversible Computation by Quantum Coherence* (arXiv:2607.06219v1, 2026), proposes classical reversible logic implemented with coherent spin dynamics in semiconductor quantum-dot arrays. Classical basis states represent inputs and outputs, while coherent spin rotations implement reversible gates (such as a DC-pulsed Ge/Si hole-spin iToffoli gate). `[DEMONSTRATED]`

  * **Energy-Accounting Discipline:** Shows that gate energy is dominated by dielectric loss in the gate-electrode capacitance from baseband pulses, and claims a calibrated cold-device gate energy below the 4 K Landauer scale. However, system-level caveats (readout, reset, refrigeration, routing, error correction) can reduce this advantage.
  * **HELIOS Relevance:** Supports the broader plausibility of physical-computing architectures where storage, transport, and computation share the same physical carrier, and where thermodynamic advantage depends on reversible operation plus explicit accounting of readout, reset, error correction, and protocol dissipation.
</ExpandableChunk>

<ExpandableChunk 
  title="Thermodynamics of Quantum Reservoir Computing (Ding & Qiu, arXiv 2026)" 
  summary="Linking reservoir predictive performance to microscopic energetic cost."
>
  Ding & Qiu, *Thermodynamics of Quantum Reservoir Computing* (arXiv:2607.02157v1, 2026), propose a non-equilibrium thermodynamic framework connecting reservoir predictive performance to microscopic energetic cost. Using Holevo quantities, they separate retained historical information from useful predictive information, treating the difference as quantum informational dissipation, and derive a generalized Landauer-style bound for continuous temporal processing. `[DEMONSTRATED]`

  * **Memory vs. Prediction:** The paper argues that a useful physical reservoir should be evaluated by the alignment between retained memory and predictive capacity, rather than raw memory capacity. Near-critical regimes improve prediction through spectral resonance, but can also increase informational dissipation by retaining non-predictive history.
  * **HELIOS Relevance:** Strengthens the validation framework for BRC reservoir evaluation, defining metrics like predictive accuracy, memory/predictive capacity, non-predictive history retention, estimated irreversible work, and readout/reset/update cost.
</ExpandableChunk>

<ExpandableChunk

  Kang et al. (ETH Zurich Research Collection 2026) demonstrate that the orbital Hall conductivity ($\sigma_{OH}$) and orbital relaxation time ($\tau_o \approx 2 \text{ ps}$) in Mn thin films are highly sensitive to structural disorder.   **HELIOS-3D Application:** To maximize the signal-to-noise ratio in our TOHE electrical detector bridge, the contact and channel materials must utilize low-disorder, highly crystalline epitaxial light metals (like Mn) rather than amorphous sputtered alloys, preventing rapid orbital current relaxation at grain boundaries. `[PROPOSED]`
